Prostate Cancer

Basics

• The prostate is a walnut-sized gland located between the bladder and the rectum. It is in charge of secreting a fluid that nourishes and protects sperm.

• About 1 in 7 men will be diagnosed with prostate cancer in their lifetime.

• Though prostate cancer is the second leading cause of cancer death in the United States, it can be treated effectively. Though 1 in 7 men are diagnosed, only 1 in 36 will die from it.

Sceening

• Men should begin to talk to their doctors about screening options beginning at the age of 40

• Annual screening is recommended for men 50 years of age and older.

• PSA Test and Digital Rectal Exam

Risk and Disparities

• African-American men have the highest rate of prostate cancer and are twice as twice as likely to die from it than their white counterparts.

• Latinos are more likely to die from prostate cancer than whites despite having a lower cancer rate.

Breast Cancer

Basics

• Approximately 232,670 women will be diagnosed with invasive breast cancer in 2014.

• Breast cancer is the second leading cause of cancer deaths for women.

• About 1 in 8 women will be diagnosed with invasive breast cancer in their lifetime.

Sceening

• Self-breast exam: Women should learn how to perform a self-breast exam by the age of 20

• Clinical breast exam (CBE)- A CBE is recommended every 3 years for women in their 20s and 30s. Women 40+ years of age are recommended to get one annually.

• Mammograms: Women 40+ years of age should get an annual mammogram.

Risk and Disparities

• Though white women are the most diagnosed group with breast cancer, African-American women are more likely to die from the disease.

• Breast cancer is the most common cancer in Korean, Chinese, Filipino and Japanese women in the U.S.

• Latinas are more likely to be diagnosed with breast cancer at a later stage than non-Latinas.

Oral Cancer

Basics

• Individuals with a history of tobacco and alcohol consumption are at high risk for developing oral cancer. Oral cancer includes cancers of the mouth and pharynx.

• Oral cancer is now most commonly caused by HPV—about 8,400 diagnosed cases per year could be due to the virus.

• Oropharynx cancers are three times more common in men that women.

Sceening

• Oral exam- A medical practitioner will look for any abornalities

Risk and Disparities

• The five year survival rate for throat cancers is much lower in African-American men (36%) than their white counterparts (61%)

Colorectal Cancer

Basics

• Colorectal cancer is found in either the colon or rectum.

• The lifetime risk of developing colorectal risk is 1 in 20 (5%).

• Approximately 50,310 death in 2014 will be due to colorectal cancer.

Sceening

• Both man and women 50 years of age and older are recommended to be screened.

Risk and Disparities

• Rates of colorectal cancer for African-American men and women are 17% higher than those for white men and women.

• Colorectal cancer is the second most diagnosed cancer in Latin@s.

Liver Cancer

Basics

• The 2014 years expects approximately 33,190 new cases to be diagnosed. Of these, 23,000 will die.

• Men are more than twice as likely to be diagnosed with liver cancer than women.

Sceening

• Screening is recommended only for those at high risk

Risk and Disparities

• Latin@s are twice as likely to be diagnosed with liver cancer compared to whites.

• Asian –Americans are 107-11.3X more susceptible to liver cancer than their white counterparts.
